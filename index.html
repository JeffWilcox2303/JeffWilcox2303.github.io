About Me

I grew up in Boylston, Massachusetts with a multitude of passions from academics (specifically math and physics), music, photography, soccer, cars, hiking, backpacking, skiing, and painting. Starting in 2014, I attended Tahanto Regional Middle/High School where I was an active member of the math competition team, the student advisory board, and student council. I applied and ended up attending MAMS which was a phenomenal experience. During my junior year of high school there, I worked on an individual STEM project based on solar cells within home windows, an app development project for job postings catered to high schoolers, and a group STEM project to build a tennis ball launcher that allows wheelchair users to actively engage with pets. During my senior year, I worked on an independent study project focused on woodworking, during which I made multiple cutting boards, a CD box, and designed a desk.

Throughout high school, I was active within Scouting BSA and earned the Eagle Scout Award in 2021, closely following my graduation. I am currently a junior at Cornell University where I serve as co-president of CSSC and contribute to the Cornell FSAE Racing team. You can find out more about my academics through my resume, here.

Outdoors

I am a man of the mountains. When I was around 3 years old, I learned how to ski around Massachusetts and Vermont, and I have stopped since. Some of my favorite mountains from recent trips include Lake Louise, Banff Sunshine, Jackson Hole, Alta, Snowbird, and Whistler Blackcomb. I also love to hike and backpack. My family has been going on hiking trips since I was 5 years old venturing to national parks such as the Grand Canyon, Arches, Bryce Canyon, Zion, Yellowstone, Olympic, Mount Rainier, Glacier, Acadia, and Pinnacles. In 2021, I went on a backpacking trip to New Mexico with the OA, an honor society within the Scouting BSA program. During this two week backpacking trip, we completed a week of service by contributing to a new trail up a mountain due to safety concerns over the original path. This was followed by a week of backpacking between campsites with backcountry music groups, rock climbing, shooting sports, and tree climbing under the stars. That trip along with my brother's encouragement led me to pick up rock climbing as a hobby and a way to keep myself active at school.
